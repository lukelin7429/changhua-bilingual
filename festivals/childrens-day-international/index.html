II.

The Promise of 1989

1989 年的那個承諾

On November 20, 1989, leaders from countries all around the world signed a special promise called the Convention on the Rights of the Child. It is the longest list of children's rights ever written down — the right to go to school, the right to play, the right to be safe, the right to have a family, the right to say what you think.

The United Nations chose November 20 as Universal Children's Day to celebrate the day grown-ups in every country made these promises to kids.

Today is also a day to remember that not every child has the same chances. Some kids are still hungry. Some still cannot go to school. So this day is not only about parties — it is about asking ourselves a real question: what can WE do to help every child, everywhere, be safe and happy?

1989 年 11 月 20 日，世界各國領袖一起簽下了一份特別的承諾——《兒童權利公約》。這是史上最長的兒童權利清單：上學、遊戲、安全、家庭、表達意見……全都是孩子的基本權利。聯合國因此把 11 月 20 日訂為「世界兒童日」。但這一天不只是慶祝——還有些孩子至今仍在挨餓、無法上學。所以這一天也提醒我們：我們能為每一個孩子做什麼？

IV.

Six Ways to Celebrate

六種慶祝世界兒童日的方式 · 注意金色標記的單字
🏫
Children's Rights Lesson
兒童權利課
What
A short class about the most important rights every child has.
When
Morning of November 20.
Why
To remember the promise the world made in 1989.
🌍
Kids in Other Countries
看看其他國家的孩子
What
Reading or watching about kids from far-away places.
When
During afternoon class.
Why
To see how kids around the world live and learn.
🤝
Saying Hello in Many Languages
用多種語言問好
What
Hola, Bonjour, こんにちは, 你好, Hello!
When
All day — during recess and class greetings.
Why
A small reminder that all kids are equal.
✏️
A Letter to a Far-Away Friend
寫信給遠方的朋友
What
Writing a short message to a pen pal or a cousin overseas.
When
As a class activity in the afternoon.
Why
To listen to what kids in other places care about too.
🎽
Wearing Blue
穿藍色
What
A small blue item — UN's color for kids' rights.
When
November 20, all day.
Why
To say "I stand with kids everywhere — they should all be safe."
🗣️
A Class Discussion
班上討論
What
Talking about: "What makes a good childhood?"
When
Last class of the day.
Why
To speak up and shape a hopeful future together.
V.

Roleplay Scripts

三個情境腳本 · 點 🔊 聽逐句範讀
SCENARIO 01
Talking About Rights at School
Goal: Discuss children's rights with a classmate.
和同學討論兒童權利。
Mia
Did you know today is World Children's Day?
Sam
Yes — teacher said every child has the right to go to school.
Mia
But not every kid in the world can. That doesn't feel equal.
Sam
That's why we have this day — so we remember to keep that promise.
SCENARIO 02
A Letter to a Friend in Another Country
Goal: Write a friendly message to a kid you've never met.
寫信給從未見過的外國朋友。
Child
Hi! My name is Anna. I live in Taiwan. What is school like in your country?
Pen Pal
Hello Anna! I'm Olu, from Nigeria. We walk one hour to school every morning.
Child
Wow, that's far. I'm hopeful that one day every school will be close to home.
Pen Pal
Me too! Let's keep writing each other. We are the future.
SCENARIO 03
Standing Up for a Quiet Classmate
Goal: Speak up kindly when someone is left out.
為被排擠的同學發聲。
Joy
Hey, why is Lin sitting alone again?
Pat
Some kids think he's weird because he's new.
Joy
That's not equal. Let's go ask him to play with us.
Pat
Good idea. We can protect him and make him feel safe.
