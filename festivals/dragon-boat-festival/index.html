II.

The Story of Qu Yuan

屈原的故事

More than two thousand years ago, in the ancient kingdom of Chu, there lived a wise and loyal poet named Qu Yuan. He loved his country and gave the king advice to keep the people safe.

But the king did not listen, and Chu was attacked by its enemies. On the 5th day of the 5th month, Qu Yuan was so heartbroken that he walked into the Miluo River and was lost forever.

The villagers loved him. They rushed to their boats and paddled as fast as they could, beating drums to scare the fish away. They threw sticky rice wrapped in bamboo leaves into the water, hoping the fish would eat the rice and not Qu Yuan. From that day on, every 5th day of the 5th month, families race dragon boats, eat zongzi, and remember a man who put his country first.

兩千多年前，楚國有一位忠誠的詩人屈原，他深愛自己的國家，多次勸諫楚王，卻不被採納。當楚國被攻陷時，他在五月初五投汨羅江以身殉國。村民們划船尋找他，敲鑼打鼓嚇走魚群，並把竹葉裹著的糯米丟入江中，希望魚兒吃飯不吃他。從那以後，每年五月初五，人們划龍舟、吃粽子，紀念這位愛國詩人。

Dragon boat
/ˈdræɡ.ən boʊt/ · n.

A long, narrow boat with a dragon head at the front.
"Each dragon boat has twenty paddlers and one drummer."
🥟
Zongzi
/ˈzɒŋ.zi/ · n.

Sticky rice with fillings, wrapped in bamboo leaves.
"Grandma makes salty pork zongzi every June."
👝
Sachet
/sæˈʃeɪ/ · n.
香包

"Children wear a sachet around their neck for good luck."
🥁
Drum
/drʌm/ · n.

"The drum keeps the paddlers in rhythm."
🎋

"Zongzi are tied with thin bamboo strings."
✍️

"Qu Yuan was a famous poet of ancient China."

IV.

Festival Highlights

六大端午習俗 · 注意金色標記的單字
🚣
Dragon Boat Race
龍舟競賽
What
Long painted boats with a drummer at the very front.
When
At noon on the 5th day of the 5th lunar month.
Why
A race that remembers the brave fishermen of Qu Yuan.
🥟
Eating Zongzi
吃粽子
What
Sticky rice with meat or beans, wrapped in bamboo leaves.
When
At every family meal during the festival.
Why
To keep the poet Qu Yuan's memory alive.
🌿
Hanging Mugwort
掛艾草
What
A bunch of mugwort and calamus tied to the door.
When
Early morning of the festival day.
Why
An ancient way to keep the home healthy and safe.
👝
Wearing a Sachet
配香包
What
A small embroidered pouch filled with sweet herbs.
When
Worn on the chest all day long.
Why
To smell good and bring steady good luck.
🥚
Standing Eggs at Noon
立蛋
What
Trying to balance a chicken egg upright on a table.
When
At exactly twelve noon, the strongest "yang" moment of the year.
Why
A brave test of patience and balance for kids.
📜
Reading Qu Yuan's Poems
讀屈原的詩
What
Families read aloud from his famous poem "Li Sao."
When
In the evening after the boat race ends.
Why
To remember a loyal voice that loved his country.
V.

Roleplay Scripts

三個情境腳本 · 點 🔊 聽逐句範讀
SCENARIO 01
At the Dragon Boat Race
Goal: Watch the race and cheer for a team using festival vocabulary.
在河岸觀看龍舟賽，為一支隊伍加油。
Amy
Look at the drums! Each dragon boat has a drummer at the front.
Ben
Yes — and listen to how the paddlers keep such a steady rhythm.
Amy
Which boat are you cheering for in this race?
Ben
The red one with the gold dragon! Let's cheer for them together!
SCENARIO 02
Wrapping Zongzi with Grandma
Goal: Learn how to wrap zongzi and discuss the festival's meaning.
和奶奶一起包粽子，聊節日的意義。
Child
Grandma, how do you fold the bamboo leaves like that?
Grandma
It takes practice. You must be patient and very gentle.
Child
Why do we eat zongzi today?
Grandma
To remember Qu Yuan, the loyal poet of long ago.
SCENARIO 03
Standing the Egg at Noon
Goal: Try the noon-egg tradition and encourage a friend.
中午試立蛋，並鼓勵同學。
Teacher
At noon, we try to stand an egg upright on the table.
Student
Is it really possible? I am scared the egg will fall.
Teacher
Be patient and brave. Hold it gently, then let go slowly.
Student
I did it! The egg is steady!
Teacher
Wonderful — you have lucky hands today.
