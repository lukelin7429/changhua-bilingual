II.

The Eight-Eight Story

八八節的由來

In Mandarin Chinese, the word for "Dad" (爸 bà) sounds almost the same as the number "8" (八 bā). So on August 8 — eight-eight — Taiwan celebrates Father's Day. It is a special day to thank Dad for his hard work, his quiet patience, and the way he looks after the family.

The idea of an "Eight-Eight Father's Day" started in Shanghai in 1945. It was a clever way to remember the day, and it spread quickly to Taiwan. In other parts of the world, Father's Day falls on different days — for example, in the United States it is the third Sunday of June. But for Taiwanese kids, "Eight-Eight" is the day to give Dad a big hug.

Today, Father's Day in Taiwan is a quiet, simple kind of celebration. Many families take Dad out for dinner, write him a card, or just spend the day together at home. Some dads work hard six days a week — so even one hour of their children's time can mean the world.

在華語裡，「爸爸」的「爸」與數字「八」同音。1945 年從上海開始，把 8 月 8 日訂為父親節，後來傳到臺灣。其他國家的父親節日期不同：美國是 6 月第三個星期日。臺灣的父親節通常很簡單——一家人吃頓晚餐、寫張卡片，或是好好陪爸爸一天。對辛苦工作一整年的爸爸來說，孩子的一小時陪伴，就是最珍貴的禮物。

Father's Day Nouns · 節慶名詞

👨
Father
/ˈfɑː.ðɚ/ · n.
父親（正式說法）
A man who has a child and takes care of them.
"My father has worked at the same factory for twenty years."
🧑
Dad
/dæd/ · n.
爸爸（口語）
An informal, friendly word kids use for their father.
"Dad always says, 'eat slowly, kids.'"
👔
Tie
/taɪ/ · n.
領帶
A long thin cloth worn under the shirt collar.
"A blue silk tie is a classic Father's Day gift."
👛
Wallet
/ˈwɑː.lɪt/ · n.
皮夾
A small case for carrying money and cards.
"Dad's old wallet has lasted him fifteen years."
🍖
Barbecue
/ˈbɑːr.bɪ.kjuː/ · n.
烤肉 · BBQ
A meal of meat and vegetables cooked over fire.
"For Father's Day, we had a backyard barbecue."
🤝
Handshake
/ˈhænd.ʃeɪk/ · n.
握手
Shaking hands as a sign of respect or greeting.
"Many Asian dads prefer a quiet handshake to a hug."

Words for a Quiet Hero · 對英雄爸爸的話

🙇
Respect
/rɪˈspekt/ · v.
尊敬
To think highly of someone for their character.
"I respect my father more than any other person."
🌟
Admire
/ədˈmaɪr/ · v.
仰慕 · 敬佩
To look up to someone for who they are.
"I admire how patient Dad is, even when he is tired."
🍽️
Treat
/triːt/ · v.

"Tonight, we are going to treat Dad to his favorite restaurant."
💪
Strong

Having physical or inner power; not giving up.

⏰
Hardworking

Proud

Feeling happy because of what someone you love has done.

IV.

Six Ways to Celebrate

六種慶祝父親節的方式 · 注意金色標記的單字
🎁
A Thoughtful Gift
用心的小禮物
What
A new tie, a wallet, or a pair of warm socks.
When
Given in the morning, before breakfast.
Why
Practical, not flashy — Taiwanese dads love a useful gift.
🍽️
Family Dinner Out
外出聚餐
What
Dinner at Dad's favorite restaurant.
When
The evening of August 8.
Why
A chance to treat Dad to a meal he didn't have to plan.
💌
Handwritten Card
手寫卡片
What
A folded paper with your real, honest words inside.
When
Given after dinner, in private.
Why
A way to tell Dad you respect what he does for the family.
🛀
A Real Day Off
一天放空
What
Mom and kids do all the housework today.
When
All day Saturday, August 8.
Why
Let the hardworking hero of the house rest fully.
🌳
A Walk in the Park
公園散步
What
A quiet walk with Dad — just talking, no phones.
When
Late afternoon when the sun is soft.
Why
Many Taiwanese dads talk best when walking side by side.
🤗
Saying Thanks Out Loud
親口說感謝
What
A real, eye-to-eye "Thank you, Dad."
When
Before going to bed.
Why
Most dads never hear it — it means more than you think.
V.

Roleplay Scripts

三個情境腳本 · 點 🔊 聽逐句範讀
SCENARIO 01
Choosing a Gift Together
Goal: Plan a small gift for Dad with a sibling.
和兄弟姊妹一起挑爸爸的禮物。
Big Sister
Father's Day is on Saturday. What should we get him?
Little Brother
A new tie? He always wears the same one to work!
Big Sister
Good idea. Or a leather wallet — his is so old.
Little Brother
Let's do both. And we can sign the card together.
Big Sister
Perfect. He will be so proud of his two helpers.
SCENARIO 02
Family Dinner Out
Goal: Treat Dad at his favorite restaurant.
在爸爸最愛的餐廳請他吃飯。
Child
Dad, this is your day. You can sit wherever you like.
Dad
Thank you, sweetheart. Sitting with you is enough for me.
Child
We picked the hot pot place — your favorite.
Dad
You know me too well.
Child
Tonight, we treat you. Order anything you want!
SCENARIO 03
Saying Thanks at Home
Goal: Say a real, honest thank-you to Dad.
親口跟爸爸說一句感謝。
Child
Dad, can we talk for a minute?
Dad
Of course. What's on your mind?
Child
I just wanted to say thank you. You are so hardworking for our family.
Dad
That's very kind of you.
Child
I admire how strong and patient you are. I want to be like you one day.
