II.

The Story of the First Thanksgiving

第一個感恩節的故事

In 1620, a small ship called the Mayflower carried 101 English settlers — we call them the Pilgrims — across the Atlantic Ocean to a new land. They landed in what is now Massachusetts, but the first winter was very hard. Half of them died from cold and hunger.

The next spring, a Native American people called the Wampanoag taught the Pilgrims how to plant corn, beans, and squash, and how to fish in the rivers. Thanks to this help, the autumn harvest of 1621 was a great success. The Pilgrims and about 90 Wampanoag people sat down together and shared a three-day feast.

For almost 250 years it was just a local celebration. Then in 1863, during the American Civil War, President Lincoln made Thanksgiving a national holiday — a yearly day to give thanks, no matter how hard the year had been. Today, families across America gather on the fourth Thursday of November to eat turkey, share gratitude, and remember the kindness of strangers.

1620 年，五月花號載著 101 位英國清教徒橫渡大西洋抵達北美。第一個冬天非常嚴酷，半數人因寒冷與飢餓死亡。隔年春天，原住民萬帕諾亞格族（Wampanoag）教他們種玉米、豆子、南瓜、捕魚。秋收豐盛，清教徒與九十位原住民共享三日盛宴。1863 年南北戰爭期間，林肯總統把這天訂為國定節日——無論這一年多艱難，都要心懷感恩。今天，全美家庭在 11 月第四個星期四聚在一起吃火雞、輪流說感謝、紀念當年那份來自陌生人的善意。

Stuffing
/ˈstʌf.ɪŋ/ · n.
填料（火雞肚裡的麵包混料）
A mix of bread, herbs, and butter cooked inside the turkey.
"Grandma's stuffing has celery, onion, and sage."
🥧
Pie
/paɪ/ · n.
派

"Pumpkin pie is the classic Thanksgiving dessert."
🌾
Harvest
/ˈhɑːr.vɪst/ · n.
豐收 · 收成
The crops that farmers gather in autumn.
"This year's harvest was bigger than we hoped."
🌟
Blessing
/ˈbles.ɪŋ/ · n.

IV.

Six Thanksgiving Traditions

六大感恩節習俗 · 注意金色標記的單字
🦃
The Family Turkey Dinner
家庭火雞晚餐
What
A whole roasted turkey in the middle of the table.
When
Late afternoon, fourth Thursday of November.
Why
To share one big meal with everyone you love.
🥧
Pumpkin & Apple Pie
南瓜派與蘋果派
What
Two classic pies, baked the morning before.
When
For dessert after the main meal.
Why
A sweet end to a cozy family evening.
🤲
Saying What You're Thankful For
輪流說感謝
What
Each person at the table says one thing they're thankful for.
When
Just before everyone starts eating.
Why
A simple way to grow gratitude in every heart.
🏈
Watching Football
看美式足球
What
Three big NFL games shown live on TV.
When
From noon till evening.
Why
A cozy, lazy way to spend time with cousins on the couch.
🎈
The Macy's Parade
梅西百貨大遊行
What
Giant balloons floating through New York City.
When
Thursday morning, live on TV.
Why
A bright start to the holiday, watched by millions.
🎒
Helping at a Food Bank
在食物銀行當志工
What
Volunteering to pack and serve meals for families in need.
When
The morning of Thanksgiving Day.
Why
To be as generous as the Wampanoag once were.
V.

Roleplay Scripts

三個情境腳本 · 點 🔊 聽逐句範讀
SCENARIO 01
Setting the Table Together
Goal: Help in the kitchen and talk about the meal.
幫忙擺餐桌、聊聊大餐。
Mom
The turkey is almost ready. Can you set the table?
Child
Sure! How many people are coming?
Mom
Eleven, including Grandma. Don't forget the pumpkin pie at the side table.
Child
Got it. The house feels so cozy tonight!
SCENARIO 02
Going Around the Table
Goal: Say one thing you are thankful for.
輪流分享今年最感謝的事。
Dad
Before we eat, let's go around. What are you thankful for this year?
Child
I am thankful for my best friend Mia. She helps me when I'm scared at school.
Dad
That's a real blessing. What else?
Child
I really appreciate Mom's cooking. And Grandma's stories every Sunday.
Dad
Beautiful. Now let's eat — before the turkey gets cold!
SCENARIO 03
Helping at the Food Bank
Goal: Volunteer to pack meals for families in need.
在食物銀行幫忙打包餐點。
Volunteer Leader
Welcome! Today we'll pack 200 dinners for families.
Student
What goes in each box?
Volunteer Leader
Rice, canned vegetables, and a small pumpkin pie. Easy.
Student
I want to share my own snack too. Is that okay?
Volunteer Leader
That's a very generous heart. Drop it in box twelve.
