Walking Out of the Classroom

走出教室，看見世界
May 8, 2026 · Grades 5–6 · 中高年級 · Two stops, one day · 兩站，一日 · Sailing-Flag school · 揚帆學校

One day, two stops — from a Beidou soybean farm to a colonial-era sugar refinery, with a wooden five-section train running between them. A class about land, food, and Taiwan's industrial memory. 一天，兩站——從北斗黃豆田到日治時代的糖廠，中間是吱呀作響的木造五分車。這是一堂關於土地、食物，與台灣工業記憶的課。

Why we walk out 為什麼要走出去

Hualong is officially certified by Taiwan's Ministry of Education as a "Sailing-Flag School" (揚帆學校) for outdoor and sustainable learning — a national badge that goes only to schools whose curriculum carries students past the classroom walls. The recognition isn't just a sticker; it's a structural commitment.

This is what we believe: a textbook can finish a sentence that real fields, real factories, and real conversations begin. So twice a semester, our middle and upper graders pack a lunch, get on a bus, and meet the world the textbook is talking about.

華龍是教育部認證的「揚帆學校」——這個全國性的肯定，只給課程把孩子帶離教室的學校。它不只是一個貼紙，而是一個結構性的承諾。

我們相信：課本是一個句子的後半段，前半段是真實的土地、真實的產業、真實的對話。所以每學期兩次，中高年級孩子帶著便當上巴士，去見見課本在講的那個世界。

The day's itinerary 這一天的行程

Morning · 上午

Tianye Diligent Learning, Beidou 田野勤學・北斗

A small educational farm built around the Taiwanese soybean — the legume your grandmother used for soy sauce, miso, tofu, and edamame, but that most kids today first meet as a frozen snack. Students walked the fields, met the farmers, and learned that "黃豆" (yellow bean) is not, in fact, yellow when it comes out of the ground.

一個以台灣黃豆為核心的教育農場——阿嬤拿來釀醬油、味噌、做豆腐、煮毛豆的那種豆子，但今天大多數孩子第一次認識它，是冷凍包裝的點心。孩子們走進田裡、遇見農夫，學到「黃豆」其實不是黃色的時候——是它還在土裡的時候。

Afternoon · 下午

Xihu Sugar Refinery 溪湖糖廠

Built in 1909 during the Japanese colonial era, the Xihu Sugar Refinery is one of Taiwan's living industrial monuments. Students rode the wooden five-section train (五分車) — so named because its track gauge is half the width of a standard rail — through cane fields the refinery once depended on, and learned why Taiwan was once called the "Sugar Kingdom."

1909 年日治時期建成，溪湖糖廠是台灣的活工業遺產之一。孩子們搭著吱呀作響的木造五分車——因為軌距只有標準軌的一半而得名——穿過糖廠當年依賴的甘蔗田，學會為什麼台灣曾經被叫做「糖業王國」。

Walking out of the classroom isn't just a visit — it's a class meeting land, culture, and history at once. 走出教室的學習，不只是一次參訪，更是一場和土地、文化、歷史相遇的課程。

What changes when a class moves 教室移動時，會改變什麼

Three things happen when a class leaves the classroom that don't happen otherwise.

First, the senses come back. Soybean smell. The metal-and-wood smell of an old train car. The temperature difference between standing in cane field shade and standing on the platform in noon sun. Children stop translating into words for a moment and just notice. That's where curiosity actually starts.

Second, the questions get specific. "Wait, the sugar train doesn't go anywhere now?" "Why did the refinery stop in 2002?" "What happens to the cane fields?" These are not questions the textbook prompts. The textbook supplies answers; the field supplies questions.

Third, the children meet adults who aren't teachers. A soybean farmer, a refinery guide, a man who used to drive the five-section train. These people don't grade them. They just talk to them. That changes how children perform — in a quietly important way.

當一堂課離開教室時，會發生三件平常不會發生的事。

第一，感官回來了。黃豆的氣味。老火車車廂的金屬與木頭味。站在甘蔗田陰影下、和站在中午月台上，溫度差異是真的。孩子停止把感受翻譯成文字，只是注意。好奇心就是從這裡開始的。

第二，問題變具體了。「欸，糖廠的小火車現在不開到別的地方嗎？」「為什麼糖廠在 2002 年停了？」「那甘蔗田後來怎麼了？」這些問題課本不會問。課本給答案；現場給問題。

第三，孩子遇到不是老師的大人。一個黃豆農夫、一個糖廠導覽員、一個曾經開過五分車的伯伯。這些人不打分數，只是跟孩子聊天。這件事悄悄但重要地，會改變孩子的表現方式。

Bringing the day back to school 把這一天帶回學校

The lesson didn't end when the bus pulled back into Hualong. The next morning, students drew what they remembered — a soybean pod, the curve of the train track, the refinery's old chimney. In Mandarin class they wrote about the moment that surprised them most. In social studies, the teacher pulled up a 1920s map of Taiwan's sugar railway network — and suddenly, the names of stations on that map were places students had been, not abstractions.

That's the whole point of being a Sailing-Flag school: the trip is the curriculum, not an interruption from it.

課程沒有在巴士開回華龍那刻結束。第二天早上，孩子們畫下自己最記得的東西——一個黃豆莢、軌道的弧度、糖廠的老煙囪。國語課裡，他們寫下最驚訝的那一個瞬間。社會課裡，老師調出 1920 年代的台灣糖業鐵路地圖——突然之間，地圖上那些車站的名字，不再是抽象的字，是孩子去過的地方。

這就是揚帆學校的核心：戶外教育是課程本身，不是課程的中斷。
