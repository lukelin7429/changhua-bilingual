When Reading Walks Out of the Classroom: A Journey Through Tainan圖書館走讀 · 知識的光在跨域走讀中閃耀

January 26, 2026 · 115/1/26

In the morning light, the reading walk began. Thanks to funding from the Confucian-Forest Lions Club, Houliao's senior-grade children set out — with President Li Mei-li and six Lions members walking alongside them like guides, a warm attention that became the loveliest light in the children's hearts.

晨光中，圖書館走讀啟程。感謝儒林獅子會經費贊助，讓後寮國小高年級的孩子們踏上這趟走讀之旅。李美麗會長與六位獅友如同引路人，陪伴我們走過知識的風景，那份溫暖的關注，是孩子們心中最美的光。

When the pages walk out of the classroom, children gain the courage to face the world.當閱讀走出教室，孩子便擁有面向世界的勇氣。

The first stop was the main branch of the Tainan Public Library, where walls of books poured down like a waterfall and every floor held a new surprise. Then the children wandered Anping Old Street, where red-brick lanes whisper the secrets of time and stone paths recall the memory of the harbour — history became something you could touch. Finally, beneath the dome of the Chimei Museum, the sculptures seemed to glow under the children's gaze.

第一站，台南市立圖書館總館，書牆如瀑布傾瀉，每一層樓都是不同的知識驚奇。接著漫步安平老街，紅磚巷弄中藏著時間的密語，石板路訴說著海港的記憶，歷史不再是課本上的文字，而是可以觸摸的溫度。最後在奇美博物館的穹頂下，藝術雕塑隨著孩子的凝望而綻放光芒。

Thanks to Director Fu and teachers Shang-lin, Yu-ren and Jun-jie for their care and guidance. This was more than a visit — it linked the words in books to the real ground beneath the children's feet, turning reading into an adventure with warmth. When the pages walk out of the classroom and the view stretches to the horizon, children gain the courage to face the world.

感謝傅主任、尚霖老師、育仁老師、俊傑老師的用心陪伴與指導。這趟旅程不只是參觀，更是將書本中的文字與腳下真實的土地脈絡產生深度連結，讓閱讀成為一場有溫度的冒險。當翻閱篇章走出教室、視野延展至遠方，孩子們便擁有了面向世界的勇氣。
